About

Two disciplines,
one throughline.

Biochemistry gave me the rigor. Policy work and community organizing gave me the reason. I keep coming back to problems where science, equity, and public health intersect.

I recently graduated with Honours Biochemistry from McMaster University, minoring in Political Science, with a 3.93 GPA and a running curiosity about where lab science actually meets people's lives — batteries that store clean energy longer, policies that keep transit and housing fair, mental health resources that reach the communities that need them most.

My research background runs through MatterLab at the University of Toronto, a self-driving lab chemistry group, where I built a dataset of iron-bipyridine posolyte complexes used to train a generative AI model for long-term battery storage. Outside the lab, I've spent years in community housing administration, youth mental health outreach, and student union policy work — always drawn to roles where I'm working directly with people, not just data.

Outside of work, I'm usually planning the next trip: whitewater kayaking, ice climbing, or paddling somewhere in Ontario. That's actually where one of the two side projects below came from.
